Nelson Mandela

The Man Who Fought for Freedom.

A Life Dedicated to Change

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944 - Joined the African National Congress (ANC) to fight against apartheid.
  • 1964 - Imprisoned for 27 years for his struggle against apartheid.
  • 1990 - Released from prison, initiated negotiations to end apartheid.
  • 1993 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1994 - Elected as President of South Africa, leading the country towards a brighter future.
  • 1999 - Steps down as President after one term, focusing on philanthropy and activism.
  • 2001 - Diagnosed with prostate cancer, continues his advocacy work.
  • 2013 - Dies at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of peace and reconciliation.

Nelson Mandela, a symbol of courage and perseverance in the face of adversity. His journey demonstrates the power of forgiveness, reconciliation, and unwavering dedication to justice and equality.
